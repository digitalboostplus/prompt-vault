# Client Communication Prompts

## Proposals & Pitching

1. **Proposal Introduction**
   Write the introduction section of a proposal for [service/product] to [client]. Set context, demonstrate understanding of their needs, and preview our solution.

2. **Scope of Work Description**
   Write a detailed scope of work section for [project/engagement]. Define deliverables, activities, timelines, and assumptions clearly.

3. **Value Proposition Statement**
   Craft a value proposition for [our offering] tailored to [client type]. Focus on outcomes they care about, not features we have.

4. **Pricing Justification**
   Write pricing justification for [price point] for [service/product]. Explain value, reference comparable options, and address potential objections.

5. **Executive Summary for Proposal**
   Write an executive summary for our proposal to [client] for [project]. Capture why us, what we'll do, and expected outcomes in under 300 words.

6. **Competitive Differentiation**
   Articulate our competitive differentiation for [client situation]. What makes us the right choice vs alternatives they're likely considering?

7. **Case Study Summary**
   Write a case study summary relevant to [prospect situation]. Cover challenge, solution, and measurable results from a similar engagement.

8. **Response to RFP Question**
   Answer this RFP question: [paste question]. Provide a comprehensive response that's specific to our capabilities and their requirements.

9. **Proposal Follow-Up**
   Write a follow-up message to [client contact] about the proposal we submitted for [project]. Add value without being pushy.

10. **Winning the Deal**
    Create talking points for a final pitch meeting with [client] for [opportunity]. Address likely concerns and differentiate from competition.

## Ongoing Client Communication

11. **Project Status Update**
    Write a status update email for [client] on [project]. Cover progress, accomplishments, any issues, and next steps in a professional but personable tone.

12. **Milestone Completion Notification**
    Notify [client] that we've completed [milestone]. Celebrate the progress, summarize what was delivered, and preview what's next.

13. **Deliverable Handoff**
    Write a message accompanying delivery of [deliverable]. Explain what's included, how to review it, and what feedback you need.

14. **Meeting Follow-Up**
    Write a follow-up email after meeting with [client] about [topics]. Summarize discussion, confirm decisions, and outline next steps.

15. **Weekly/Monthly Report Cover**
    Write a cover message for the [weekly/monthly] report to [client]. Highlight key points they should focus on and offer to discuss.

16. **Proactive Update**
    Write a proactive update to [client] about something they should know: [describe situation]. Inform them before they have to ask.

17. **Checking In**
    Write a check-in message to [client] during a quiet period in our engagement. Maintain the relationship without being intrusive.

18. **Sharing Industry Insights**
    Write an email sharing relevant industry news/insights with [client]. Add value and demonstrate ongoing attention to their industry.

19. **Holiday/Seasonal Message**
    Write a [holiday/seasonal] message to clients. Be genuine and professional, not generic or overly salesy.

20. **Relationship Maintenance**
    Write an email to [past client] to maintain the relationship. Reference our past work together and offer value without an immediate ask.

## Managing Expectations

21. **Setting Expectations Upfront**
    Write talking points for setting expectations with [new client] for [engagement]. Cover what to expect, how we work, and communication norms.

22. **Clarifying Scope**
    Address a scope misunderstanding with [client]. Clarify what's included/excluded professionally while maintaining the relationship.

23. **Managing Timeline Expectations**
    Communicate to [client] about realistic timeline for [deliverable/project]. Address their urgency while setting achievable expectations.

24. **Explaining Process**
    Explain our [process/methodology] to [client]. Make it understandable, emphasize benefits to them, and set expectations for their involvement.

25. **Resetting Expectations**
    Write a message to [client] to reset expectations about [aspect of engagement]. Handle diplomatically while being clear.

26. **Communicating Constraints**
    Explain constraints that affect [client project] to [client]. Be honest about limitations while maintaining confidence in delivery.

27. **Out-of-Scope Request Response**
    Respond to [client]'s request for [out-of-scope work]. Acknowledge the request, explain scope boundaries, and offer alternatives.

28. **Change Order Proposal**
    Write a change order proposal for [additional work] for [client]. Explain what's needed, why, and impact on timeline/cost.

29. **Quality vs Speed Tradeoff**
    Discuss tradeoffs with [client] regarding [quality vs speed situation]. Present options and guide toward best decision.

30. **Risk Communication**
    Communicate a project risk to [client]: [describe risk]. Frame appropriately, explain what we're doing about it, and maintain confidence.

## Difficult Situations

31. **Delivering Bad News**
    Deliver bad news to [client] about [situation - missed deadline, budget overrun, issue]. Be direct, take responsibility appropriately, and focus on solutions.

32. **Apologizing for Mistake**
    Write an apology to [client] for [our mistake]. Acknowledge the impact, take ownership, explain what we're doing to fix it, and prevent recurrence.

33. **Responding to Complaint**
    Respond to [client]'s complaint about [issue]. Acknowledge their frustration, address the issue specifically, and work toward resolution.

34. **Price Increase Notification**
    Notify [client] of price increase from [current] to [new] effective [date]. Explain reasoning, emphasize continued value, and offer to discuss.

35. **Declining Work**
    Decline a project/request from [client]. Be professional, honest about why (if appropriate), and preserve the relationship.

36. **Pushing Back on Client**
    Push back on [client]'s request/direction that I believe is wrong. Be respectful but clear, and offer alternative recommendations.

37. **Addressing Non-Payment**
    Write a professional message to [client] about overdue payment. Progress through reminder stages while maintaining relationship.

38. **Contract Dispute**
    Address a contract interpretation disagreement with [client]. Seek resolution while protecting our interests.

39. **Ending an Engagement**
    Handle ending engagement with [client] who is [difficult/not a fit]. Be professional, fulfill obligations, and exit gracefully.

40. **Crisis Communication**
    Communicate with [client] during [crisis situation]. Provide timely updates, show we're on it, and maintain trust.

## Building Relationships

41. **New Client Welcome**
    Write a welcome message to [new client] at start of engagement. Set positive tone, express enthusiasm, and establish communication rhythm.

42. **Introducing Team Member**
    Introduce [team member] to [client]. Explain their role, build confidence in their capabilities, and facilitate good working relationship.

43. **Thanking Client**
    Write a genuine thank-you to [client] for [specific reason]. Be specific and sincere, not generic.

44. **Celebrating Success**
    Celebrate a shared success with [client]: [describe success]. Acknowledge their contributions and express appreciation.

45. **Asking for Feedback**
    Request feedback from [client] on [engagement/deliverable]. Make it easy for them and show genuine interest in improving.

46. **Requesting Referral**
    Ask [satisfied client] for referrals. Time it right, make it easy, and don't be pushy.

47. **Requesting Testimonial**
    Request a testimonial from [client]. Explain why it matters, make it easy, and offer to draft something they can edit.

48. **Upsell/Cross-sell Introduction**
    Introduce [additional service/product] to [existing client]. Lead with their benefit, not our sales goal.

49. **Contract Renewal**
    Initiate contract renewal discussion with [client]. Highlight value delivered and discuss any adjustments for renewal period.

50. **Long-Term Partnership Discussion**
    Open discussion with [client] about evolving to a long-term partnership. Frame the opportunity and benefits for both parties.
