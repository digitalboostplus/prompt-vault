# Email Communication Prompts

## Cold Outreach & Introductions

1. **First Contact Introduction**
   Write a professional cold email introducing myself and my company to [prospect name] at [company]. I offer [service/product]. Keep it under 150 words, focus on one specific pain point they likely have, and include a clear but low-pressure call to action.

2. **Warm Introduction Request**
   Draft an email asking [mutual connection] for an introduction to [target person] at [company]. Explain why I want to connect without being pushy, and make it easy for them to forward or copy/paste an intro.

3. **Following Up After No Response**
   Write a follow-up email to [prospect] who hasn't responded to my initial outreach. Reference my previous email briefly, add new value or insight, and give them an easy way to say no if not interested.

4. **Re-engagement After Long Silence**
   Create an email to reconnect with [contact name] whom I haven't spoken to in [timeframe]. Reference our previous interaction naturally and provide a relevant reason to reconnect now.

5. **Conference/Event Pre-Outreach**
   Write an email to [person] letting them know I'll be at [event/conference] and would like to meet. Suggest a specific time/place and explain briefly why meeting would be mutually valuable.

## Professional Correspondence

6. **Meeting Request Email**
   Draft a meeting request email to [recipient]. I need to discuss [topic]. Propose 2-3 specific time slots and keep the email concise while explaining why this meeting matters.

7. **Declining a Meeting Politely**
   Write an email declining a meeting request from [person] about [topic]. Be respectful, provide a brief honest reason, and if appropriate, suggest an alternative (email summary, delegate, future timing).

8. **Rescheduling a Meeting**
   Create an email to reschedule my meeting with [person] originally set for [date/time]. Apologize appropriately, explain briefly, and offer 2-3 alternative times.

9. **Thank You After Meeting**
   Write a thank-you email to [person] after our meeting about [topic]. Reference 1-2 specific points discussed, confirm any next steps agreed upon, and keep it genuine not generic.

10. **Introduction to New Team Member**
    Draft an email introducing [new hire name] to the team. Include their role, relevant background, what they'll be working on, and encourage people to welcome them.

## Difficult Conversations

11. **Delivering Bad News to Client**
    Write an email to [client name] informing them that [bad news - deadline miss, price increase, service issue]. Be direct, take appropriate responsibility, explain what we're doing about it, and maintain the relationship.

12. **Addressing Performance Issue**
    Create an email to [employee name] addressing [specific performance issue]. Be factual, reference specific examples, express support for improvement, and set clear expectations.

13. **Apologizing for a Mistake**
    Write an email apologizing to [recipient] for [specific mistake]. Take ownership, briefly explain what happened without making excuses, describe what we're doing to fix it and prevent recurrence.

14. **Pushing Back on Unreasonable Request**
    Draft an email responding to [person]'s request for [unreasonable ask]. Decline professionally, explain constraints briefly, and if possible offer an alternative that works for both parties.

15. **Addressing a Misunderstanding**
    Write an email to [person] to clear up a misunderstanding about [topic]. Clarify my position without being defensive, acknowledge their perspective, and move toward resolution.

## Internal Communications

16. **Project Status Update**
    Create a status update email for [project name] to send to stakeholders. Cover what's completed, what's in progress, any blockers or risks, and next milestones. Keep it scannable.

17. **Request for Resources**
    Write an email to [manager/stakeholder] requesting additional [resources - budget, headcount, time]. Make a clear business case with specific numbers and expected outcomes.

18. **Announcing a Process Change**
    Draft an email announcing [new process/policy] to the team. Explain what's changing, why, when it takes effect, and where to find more information or ask questions.

19. **Asking for Deadline Extension**
    Create an email to [stakeholder] requesting an extension on [deliverable]. Explain the situation honestly, propose a new realistic deadline, and describe what you're doing to get back on track.

20. **Cross-Department Collaboration Request**
    Write an email to [person in other department] requesting their help with [task/project]. Explain context, what you need from them specifically, and timeline/priority.

## Client & Vendor Relations

21. **Proposal Follow-Up**
    Write a follow-up email to [client] regarding the proposal I sent on [date]. Add value without being pushy, address potential concerns proactively, and make next steps clear.

22. **Contract Renewal Discussion**
    Create an email to [client] initiating discussion about renewing our contract. Highlight value delivered, mention any updates to terms, and express genuine interest in continuing.

23. **Price Increase Notification**
    Draft an email notifying [client] of a price increase from [current] to [new price] effective [date]. Explain the reason, emphasize continued value, and offer to discuss.

24. **Vendor Negotiation**
    Write an email to [vendor] negotiating [specific terms - price, timeline, scope]. Be professional but clear about what we need, and leave room for discussion.

25. **Ending a Business Relationship**
    Create an email to [vendor/client] ending our business relationship professionally. Be direct but respectful, handle logistics, and leave the door open for future opportunities.

## Requests & Asks

26. **Requesting a Referral**
    Write an email to [satisfied client/contact] asking if they know anyone who might benefit from [service/product]. Make it easy for them to refer, and don't be pushy.

27. **Asking for a Testimonial**
    Draft an email to [client] requesting a testimonial about working with us. Reference specific positive outcomes, make it easy (offer to draft something they can edit), and respect if they decline.

28. **Requesting Feedback**
    Create an email to [recipient] asking for candid feedback on [project, presentation, proposal]. Be specific about what feedback would be most helpful.

29. **Asking for a Favor**
    Write an email to [contact] asking for [specific favor]. Be clear about what you need, why you're asking them specifically, and make it easy to say no.

30. **Following Up on Unanswered Request**
    Draft a follow-up email about [previous request] that hasn't received a response. Be brief, assume positive intent, and make responding as easy as possible.

## Announcements & Updates

31. **Company News Announcement**
    Write an email announcing [company news - new product, partnership, achievement] to [audience]. Balance excitement with professionalism, include relevant details, and clear next steps if any.

32. **Personnel Change Announcement**
    Create an email announcing that [person] is [leaving/joining/changing roles]. Handle sensitively, provide transition information, and maintain morale.

33. **Service/Product Update**
    Draft an email to customers about [update/change to product or service]. Explain what's changing, why it benefits them, when, and where to learn more.

34. **Event Invitation**
    Write an email inviting [recipients] to [event]. Include all essential details (what, when, where, RSVP), convey value of attending, and make registration easy.

35. **Newsletter Content**
    Create engaging newsletter content for [company] covering [topics]. Include a compelling opener, 2-3 content sections with value, and a clear CTA.

## Response Templates

36. **Responding to Sales Pitch**
    Write a polite but firm email declining a sales pitch from [company/person]. Don't leave false hope, but don't burn bridges either.

37. **Acknowledging Receipt**
    Create a brief email acknowledging receipt of [document, application, inquiry] and setting expectations for next steps and timeline.

38. **Out of Office Message**
    Write a professional out-of-office auto-reply for [reason - vacation, conference, leave]. Include dates, urgency instructions, and who to contact.

39. **Responding to Complaint**
    Draft a response to a customer complaint about [issue]. Acknowledge their frustration, take ownership where appropriate, explain resolution, and rebuild trust.

40. **Information Request Response**
    Write an email responding to [person]'s request for information about [topic]. Provide clear, complete information and offer to answer follow-up questions.

## Networking & Relationship Building

41. **Post-Event Follow-Up**
    Create a follow-up email to [person] I met at [event]. Reference our conversation specifically, suggest a way to continue the relationship, and be memorable.

42. **LinkedIn Connection to Email**
    Write an email to [LinkedIn connection] to take the relationship offline. Explain why you'd like to connect further and suggest a specific next step.

43. **Congratulations Message**
    Draft an email congratulating [person] on [achievement - promotion, award, milestone]. Be genuine, specific, and don't use it as a sales opportunity.

44. **Checking In**
    Write a simple check-in email to [contact] to maintain the relationship. Add value (share an article, insight) without asking for anything.

45. **Introducing Two Contacts**
    Create a double opt-in introduction email connecting [Person A] and [Person B]. Explain to each why I think they should know each other.

## Specific Situations

46. **Requesting Invoice Payment**
    Write a professional email following up on unpaid invoice #[number] now [X] days overdue. Be firm but maintain the relationship.

47. **Submitting Expense Report**
    Draft an email submitting an expense report for [period/project] with attached documentation. Summarize total and note any items needing explanation.

48. **Requesting Time Off**
    Write an email to [manager] requesting time off from [dates]. Mention coverage plan and any deadlines that might be affected.

49. **Announcing Remote/Office Schedule**
    Create an email informing team of my working schedule for [period]. Include how to reach me and any availability constraints.

50. **Exit/Transition Email**
    Write a professional farewell email to colleagues as I [leave the company/change roles]. Express gratitude, share transition info, and how to stay in touch.
