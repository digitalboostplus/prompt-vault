# Meeting Management Prompts

## Agenda Creation

1. **Standard Team Meeting Agenda**
   Create an agenda for a [duration] team meeting. Topics to cover: [list topics]. Include time allocations for each item, identify who leads each section, and leave room for open discussion.

2. **One-on-One Meeting Agenda**
   Generate a one-on-one meeting agenda with [direct report/manager]. Focus on: current projects, blockers, professional development, and feedback (both directions). Keep it conversational but productive.

3. **Project Kickoff Agenda**
   Create a comprehensive kickoff meeting agenda for [project name]. Cover project overview, goals and success metrics, roles and responsibilities, timeline, risks, and next steps.

4. **Quarterly Business Review Agenda**
   Draft a QBR agenda for [client/internal team]. Include performance review, wins and challenges, upcoming plans, and strategic discussion points. Target [duration].

5. **Brainstorming Session Agenda**
   Design an agenda for a brainstorming session on [topic/challenge]. Include warm-up, problem framing, ideation phases, evaluation criteria, and next steps. Build in energy management.

6. **Board Meeting Agenda**
   Create a board meeting agenda covering [topics]. Include time for CEO update, financial review, strategic discussions, and governance items. Follow proper board meeting protocol.

7. **All-Hands Meeting Agenda**
   Draft an all-hands meeting agenda for [company/department]. Balance company updates with employee engagement, recognition, and Q&A time.

8. **Workshop Agenda**
   Create a [duration] workshop agenda for [topic]. Include learning objectives, interactive activities, breaks, and clear outcomes participants will achieve.

9. **Retrospective Meeting Agenda**
   Design a retrospective agenda for [project/sprint]. Structure for what went well, what could improve, and action items. Keep it blameless and constructive.

10. **Strategy Planning Session Agenda**
    Create an agenda for a [duration] strategy session on [topic]. Include current state analysis, future vision discussion, gap identification, and priority setting.

## Meeting Summaries & Notes

11. **Executive Meeting Summary**
    Summarize this meeting for executives: [paste notes or key points]. Focus on decisions made, action items with owners, and strategic implications. Keep it under 200 words.

12. **Detailed Meeting Minutes**
    Create formal meeting minutes from these notes: [paste notes]. Include attendees, agenda items covered, discussion points, decisions, and action items with due dates.

13. **Action Items Extraction**
    Review these meeting notes and extract all action items: [paste notes]. Format as a clear list with owner, task, and deadline for each.

14. **Client Meeting Summary**
    Summarize this client meeting for internal stakeholders: [paste notes]. Include client sentiment, key requests, commitments made, and follow-up needed.

15. **Decision Log Entry**
    Document this decision from our meeting: [describe decision]. Include context, options considered, rationale for choice, and any dissenting views noted.

## Meeting Facilitation

16. **Discussion Question Generator**
    Generate 5-7 discussion questions for a meeting about [topic]. Make them open-ended, thought-provoking, and structured to build on each other.

17. **Meeting Ground Rules**
    Create a set of ground rules for [type of meeting/team]. Cover participation, technology use, decision-making, and time management. Keep them memorable.

18. **Icebreaker Questions**
    Generate 5 appropriate icebreaker questions for a [type of meeting] with [audience description]. Keep them professional but engaging, and quick to answer.

19. **Time Check Prompts**
    Create transition phrases to help keep a meeting on schedule. Include polite ways to cut off tangents, move to next topics, and table items for later.

20. **Parking Lot Management**
    Explain how to effectively manage a "parking lot" for off-topic items in meetings. Include when to use it, how to capture items, and follow-up process.

## Pre-Meeting Preparation

21. **Meeting Preparation Checklist**
    Create a preparation checklist for [type of meeting]. Include logistics, materials needed, pre-reads to send, and mental/strategic preparation.

22. **Stakeholder Briefing**
    Write a pre-meeting briefing for [stakeholder] attending [meeting type]. Cover context, their role, key points to listen for, and any sensitivities.

23. **Pre-Read Summary**
    Summarize this document into a 2-minute pre-read for [meeting]: [paste or describe document]. Highlight what attendees must understand before the meeting.

24. **Objectives Clarification**
    Help me clarify the objectives for my meeting about [topic]. What should we specifically decide, learn, or align on? What does success look like?

25. **Attendee List Review**
    Review this attendee list for [meeting purpose]: [list attendees]. Identify if we're missing anyone critical, if anyone is optional, and potential dynamics to manage.

## Virtual Meeting Best Practices

26. **Virtual Meeting Guidelines**
    Create guidelines for effective virtual meetings for our team. Cover video on/off, muting, chat usage, screen sharing, and engagement techniques.

27. **Hybrid Meeting Protocol**
    Develop a protocol for hybrid meetings (some in-room, some remote). Address equity for remote participants, technology needs, and facilitation adjustments.

28. **Screen Share Preparation**
    Create a checklist for preparing to share my screen in an important meeting. Cover technical, content, and professional appearance considerations.

29. **Virtual Meeting Engagement Tactics**
    Suggest 5-7 tactics to improve engagement in a virtual meeting about [topic] with [number] participants. Include ways to use technology effectively.

30. **Technical Difficulties Script**
    Write scripts for common virtual meeting issues: joining late, audio problems, needing to leave early, and handling interruptions professionally.

## Specific Meeting Types

31. **Sales Demo Structure**
    Create a structure for a [duration] sales demo of [product/service] to [prospect type]. Include discovery, presentation, objection handling, and close.

32. **Interview Panel Meeting**
    Design a [duration] interview for [role] with [number] interviewers. Assign focus areas to each interviewer, suggest questions, and include evaluation criteria.

33. **Performance Review Meeting**
    Structure a performance review meeting with [employee]. Cover achievements, areas for growth, goals, compensation (if applicable), and career development.

34. **Conflict Resolution Meeting**
    Design a meeting structure to resolve conflict between [parties] about [issue]. Include neutral framing, structured listening, and path to resolution.

35. **Vendor Evaluation Meeting**
    Create an agenda for evaluating [vendor/solution]. Include demo review, Q&A areas, scoring criteria, and team alignment on decision process.

36. **Crisis Response Meeting**
    Structure an emergency meeting to address [crisis/issue]. Prioritize information gathering, decision-making, action assignment, and communication planning.

37. **Training Session Structure**
    Design a training session on [topic] for [audience]. Include learning objectives, content delivery, practice activities, and knowledge check.

38. **Project Post-Mortem Agenda**
    Create a post-mortem agenda for [completed project]. Structure for honest assessment of what worked, what didn't, and lessons to carry forward.

39. **Budget Review Meeting**
    Structure a budget review meeting for [department/project]. Cover current spend, variances, forecasts, and any reallocation requests.

40. **Town Hall Q&A Preparation**
    Prepare for Q&A at a town hall about [topic/announcement]. Anticipate likely questions, prepare responses, and plan for difficult questions.

## Meeting Efficiency

41. **Meeting Necessity Check**
    Analyze if this meeting is necessary: [describe proposed meeting]. Suggest if it could be an email, async discussion, or smaller group. If meeting is needed, suggest optimal format.

42. **Meeting Length Optimizer**
    This meeting currently takes [current duration]. Help me restructure it to [target duration] while achieving the same outcomes. Identify what can be cut or done async.

43. **Recurring Meeting Audit**
    Audit this recurring meeting: [describe meeting]. Determine if it's still needed, optimal frequency, right attendees, and if format should change.

44. **Meeting-Free Time Blocks**
    Help me design a meeting-free time policy for my team. Consider what blocks to protect, exceptions, and how to communicate and enforce it.

45. **Decision-Making Acceleration**
    We keep having meetings about [topic] without deciding. Create a structure for our next meeting that will force a decision. Include pre-work required.

## Follow-Up & Accountability

46. **Follow-Up Email Template**
    Create a follow-up email template for after [meeting type]. Include summary, action items, deadlines, and next meeting details if applicable.

47. **Action Item Tracker**
    Design an action item tracking system for our team meetings. Include capture method, visibility, reminders, and accountability review process.

48. **Commitment Verification**
    Write follow-up language to verify commitments made in meetings. Make it helpful not nagging, and appropriate for [relationship context].

49. **Progress Check-In Structure**
    Create a quick check-in structure to review progress on meeting action items. Keep it efficient and focused on blockers.

50. **Meeting Effectiveness Survey**
    Design a brief survey to assess meeting effectiveness. Cover value of time spent, preparation, facilitation, and outcomes. Keep it to 5 questions max.
