# Negotiation & Sales Prompts

## Sales Conversations

1. **Discovery Call Script**
   Create a discovery call script for [product/service] with [prospect type]. Include opening, qualifying questions, pain point exploration, and next steps.

2. **Qualifying Questions**
   Generate 10 qualifying questions for [product/service] sales. Cover budget, authority, need, and timeline (BANT) plus strategic fit.

3. **Pain Point Questions**
   Create questions to uncover pain points for prospects considering [product/service]. Go deep on impact and urgency.

4. **Value Proposition Delivery**
   Write a compelling value proposition delivery for [product/service] tailored to [prospect type]. Focus on outcomes, not features.

5. **Demo Script**
   Create a demo script for [product/service] for [prospect type]. Structure for engagement, address likely concerns, and build toward close.

6. **Sales Presentation Outline**
   Structure a sales presentation for [product/service] to [audience]. Include attention grabber, pain points, solution, proof, and ask.

7. **Proposal Walkthrough**
   Create talking points for walking [prospect] through our proposal for [project/solution]. Emphasize value and handle price discussion.

8. **Sales Follow-Up Sequence**
   Create a follow-up sequence after [sales touchpoint]. Include timing, channel, and message for each touch.

9. **Re-Engaging Cold Leads**
   Write an outreach message to re-engage [cold leads] who went quiet [timeframe] ago. Provide new value and reason to reconnect.

10. **Closing Techniques**
    Suggest closing techniques appropriate for [sales situation]. Include language for trial close, assumptive close, and direct ask.

## Objection Handling

11. **Price Objection Response**
    Write responses to "your price is too high" for [product/service]. Include reframing, value reinforcement, and negotiation options.

12. **Competitor Comparison Objection**
    Handle the objection "[competitor] offers similar for less." Address without badmouthing, emphasize differentiation.

13. **Timing Objection Response**
    Respond to "now isn't the right time" objection. Understand real barrier and create urgency appropriately.

14. **Authority Objection Handling**
    Handle "I need to check with my [boss/team/partner]." Get commitment and set up for successful internal selling.

15. **Status Quo Objection**
    Address "we're fine with our current solution." Help them see hidden costs and missed opportunities.

16. **Trust/Risk Objection**
    Handle concerns about risk of working with us: "You're too small/new/unknown." Build confidence with proof and risk reduction.

17. **Feature Missing Objection**
    Respond to "you don't have [specific feature]." Understand importance, offer alternatives or roadmap.

18. **Budget Already Allocated**
    Handle "we've already allocated our budget." Explore flexibility, staged approach, or next budget cycle strategy.

19. **Bad Past Experience Objection**
    Address "we tried something similar and it didn't work." Understand what failed and differentiate our approach.

20. **Objection Prevention**
    Identify likely objections for [product/service] to [prospect type] and suggest how to address proactively in the sales process.

## Negotiation Preparation

21. **Negotiation Planning**
    Help me plan for negotiation with [party] about [subject]. Define goals, BATNA, reservation point, and strategy.

22. **BATNA Development**
    Help me develop and strengthen my BATNA for negotiation with [party] over [issue]. What are my alternatives?

23. **Understanding Their Side**
    Help me understand [other party]'s perspective in our negotiation over [issue]. What are their likely interests and constraints?

24. **Negotiation Opening**
    Prepare my opening position for negotiation on [subject] with [party]. Set appropriate anchor while remaining credible.

25. **Concession Planning**
    Plan my concession strategy for [negotiation]. What can I trade, in what order, to maximize value while reaching agreement?

26. **Win-Win Framing**
    Reframe this negotiation from positional to interest-based: [describe situation]. Identify shared interests and creative solutions.

27. **Negotiation Questions**
    Generate questions to ask in my negotiation with [party] about [subject]. Gather information and understand their priorities.

28. **Red Line Definition**
    Help me define my red lines for [negotiation]. What am I truly unwilling to accept?

29. **Leverage Assessment**
    Assess leverage in negotiation between [us] and [them] over [subject]. Who has power and from what sources?

30. **Negotiation Risk Assessment**
    Identify risks in this negotiation: [describe situation]. Include strategic, relationship, and execution risks.

## Negotiation Tactics

31. **Price Negotiation Tactics**
    Suggest tactics for negotiating price with [party] for [product/service]. Include when to hold firm vs when to flex.

32. **Contract Term Negotiation**
    Prepare for negotiating these contract terms: [list terms]. Prioritize, develop positions, and identify trade-offs.

33. **Handling Hardball Tactics**
    How should I respond if [party] uses these tactics: [describe tactics - lowball, deadline pressure, etc.]. Keep negotiation productive.

34. **Breaking Impasse**
    We're stuck in negotiation over [issue]. Suggest approaches to break the impasse while protecting my interests.

35. **Multi-Party Negotiation**
    Prepare for negotiation involving [multiple parties]. Consider coalitions, competing interests, and sequencing.

36. **Negotiating as Seller**
    Tactics for negotiating from seller position for [product/service] with [buyer type]. Protect value while closing deal.

37. **Negotiating as Buyer**
    Tactics for negotiating as buyer of [product/service] from [seller type]. Get best value without damaging relationship.

38. **Deadline Negotiation**
    Negotiate timeline/deadline with [party] for [deliverable]. Balance their needs with realistic constraints.

39. **Scope Negotiation**
    Negotiate scope of [project/engagement] with [party]. Handle scope creep pressure while preserving relationship.

40. **Salary Negotiation Prep**
    Prepare for salary negotiation with [employer/prospect]. Include research, positioning, and counter-offer strategy.

## Closing & Post-Sale

41. **Trial Close Questions**
    Create trial close questions for [sales situation]. Test readiness without pressure.

42. **Asking for the Business**
    Write scripts for directly asking for the business in [sales situation]. Clear, confident, not pushy.

43. **Contract Negotiation Summary**
    Summarize negotiated terms for [agreement] for stakeholder approval. Cover key terms, what we gave/got, and recommendation.

44. **Deal Documentation**
    Draft key terms for [agreement type] with [party]. Cover scope, pricing, timeline, and other essential terms.

45. **Post-Close Communication**
    Write post-sale communication to [new customer]. Confirm decision, set expectations, and begin relationship building.

46. **Handoff to Success/Delivery**
    Create internal handoff documentation for [closed deal]. Include context, customer expectations, and key stakeholder info.

47. **Lost Deal Follow-Up**
    Write follow-up to [prospect] who chose [competitor/alternative]. Leave door open and gather intelligence.

48. **Upsell Conversation**
    Structure upsell conversation with [existing customer] for [additional product/service]. Lead with their value, not our revenue.

49. **Renewal Negotiation**
    Prepare for renewal negotiation with [customer]. Address potential concerns and strengthen partnership.

50. **Reference Request**
    Ask [satisfied customer] to serve as reference for [prospect]. Make the ask easy and time-bound.
