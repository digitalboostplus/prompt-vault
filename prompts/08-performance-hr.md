# Performance & HR Prompts

## Performance Reviews

1. **Self-Assessment**
   Write a self-assessment for my performance review covering [period]. Include accomplishments, challenges faced, skills developed, and areas for growth.

2. **Manager Review Comments**
   Write performance review comments for [employee] who has [describe performance level]. Balance feedback with recognition and include specific examples.

3. **Exceeds Expectations Review**
   Write a performance review for [employee] who exceeded expectations. Recognize achievements, provide meaningful development feedback, and set future goals.

4. **Meets Expectations Review**
   Write a performance review for [employee] who meets expectations. Acknowledge solid performance, identify growth opportunities, and motivate improvement.

5. **Below Expectations Review**
   Write a performance review for [employee] who is below expectations. Be clear about gaps, provide specific examples, and outline improvement expectations.

6. **Performance Improvement Plan**
   Create a performance improvement plan for [employee] with [performance issues]. Include specific expectations, support provided, timeline, and consequences.

7. **360 Feedback Request**
   Write a 360 feedback request for [employee]'s review. Ask specific questions that will generate useful input on [areas to assess].

8. **Providing 360 Feedback**
   Provide 360 feedback for [colleague]. Cover strengths, areas for development, and specific examples to illustrate points.

9. **Goal Setting Discussion Guide**
   Create a guide for a goal-setting discussion with [employee]. Include questions to ask and how to align their goals with team objectives.

10. **Mid-Year Check-In**
    Structure a mid-year check-in with [employee]. Cover progress on goals, challenges, support needed, and course corrections.

## Feedback & Coaching

11. **Positive Feedback**
    Write positive feedback for [employee] about [specific achievement/behavior]. Be specific, explain impact, and encourage continued performance.

12. **Constructive Feedback**
    Deliver constructive feedback to [employee] about [issue]. Be specific, assume positive intent, and focus on behavior not person.

13. **Feedback Conversation Script**
    Create a script for a feedback conversation with [employee] about [topic]. Include opening, core message, listening points, and next steps.

14. **Coaching Conversation**
    Structure a coaching conversation with [employee] about [development area]. Ask questions to help them discover insights rather than just telling.

15. **Career Development Discussion**
    Prepare for a career development discussion with [employee]. Explore their aspirations, assess skills gaps, and discuss development path.

16. **Skill Gap Analysis**
    Analyze skill gaps for [employee] in their current role or for [target role]. Identify specific gaps and development recommendations.

17. **Mentoring Session Structure**
    Structure a mentoring session with [mentee]. Balance their agenda with proactive guidance and actionable takeaways.

18. **Recognition Message**
    Write a recognition message for [employee] about [achievement]. Make it specific, meaningful, and appropriate for sharing.

19. **Team Recognition**
    Write recognition for [team] for [accomplishment]. Acknowledge collective effort while highlighting key contributors.

20. **Praise Email to Skip Level**
    Write an email to [manager's manager] recognizing [employee]'s excellent work on [project/achievement].

## Hiring & Onboarding

21. **Job Description**
    Write a job description for [role]. Include responsibilities, requirements, nice-to-haves, and compelling language about the opportunity.

22. **Interview Question Set**
    Create interview questions for [role] assessing [competencies]. Include behavioral questions with probing follow-ups.

23. **Technical Interview Questions**
    Create technical interview questions for [role]. Cover necessary skills with questions that differentiate skill levels.

24. **Interview Evaluation**
    Write an interview evaluation for [candidate] for [role]. Summarize strengths, concerns, and hiring recommendation with evidence.

25. **Offer Letter Content**
    Write offer letter content for [role] with [compensation details]. Balance professional with welcoming tone.

26. **Rejection Email**
    Write a rejection email to [candidate] for [role]. Be respectful, provide appropriate feedback if applicable, and leave door open.

27. **Onboarding Plan**
    Create a 30-60-90 day onboarding plan for [role]. Include learning objectives, key meetings, milestones, and success criteria.

28. **First Week Schedule**
    Design a first week schedule for [new hire] in [role]. Balance orientation with role-specific activities and relationship building.

29. **New Hire Announcement**
    Write a team announcement introducing [new hire]. Include their background, role, and encouragement to welcome them.

30. **New Hire Check-In Questions**
    Create check-in questions for [new hire] at [30/60/90] days. Assess their experience, integration, and early performance.

## Team Management

31. **Team Meeting Agenda**
    Create a team meeting agenda for [team size] meeting [frequency]. Balance updates with engagement and problem-solving.

32. **Team Norms**
    Draft team norms for [team]. Cover communication, decision-making, meetings, and collaboration expectations.

33. **Delegation Framework**
    Create a framework for delegating [types of work] to [team members]. Include decision criteria and support levels.

34. **Team Building Activity**
    Suggest a team building activity for [team description] that addresses [goals - trust, communication, fun]. Consider remote/in-person.

35. **Addressing Team Conflict**
    Plan an approach to address conflict between [team members] about [issue]. Include conversation structure and resolution steps.

36. **Low Performer Conversation**
    Prepare for a conversation with [employee] about their performance issues: [describe]. Include clear expectations and support offer.

37. **Retaining High Performer**
    Create a retention plan for [high performer] who may be at risk of leaving. Address likely motivations and concerns.

38. **Promotion Justification**
    Write a promotion justification for [employee] to [new level/title]. Document achievements, readiness, and impact.

39. **Salary Increase Request**
    Write a salary increase request for [employee]. Include market data, performance evidence, and retention considerations.

40. **Team Restructuring Communication**
    Write communication about team restructuring: [describe changes]. Address impacts, rationale, and answer anticipated questions.

## Difficult HR Situations

41. **Termination Talking Points**
    Create talking points for terminating [employee] for [reason]. Be clear, humane, and legally appropriate.

42. **Layoff Notification**
    Write a layoff notification for [employee]. Handle with dignity, explain why, and cover logistics.

43. **Resignation Response**
    Respond to [employee]'s resignation. Handle professionally, whether trying to retain or accepting the departure.

44. **Exit Interview Questions**
    Create exit interview questions for [departing employee]. Gather useful insights while keeping conversation appropriate.

45. **Documenting Performance Issues**
    Write documentation for [employee]'s performance issue: [describe]. Be factual, specific, and appropriate for HR files.

46. **HR Policy Communication**
    Communicate new HR policy on [topic] to employees. Explain what, why, and how it affects them.

47. **Accommodation Request Response**
    Respond to [employee]'s accommodation request for [need]. Balance compliance with operational requirements.

48. **Complaint Investigation**
    Outline steps for investigating a complaint from [employee] about [issue]. Ensure fair, thorough process.

49. **Return to Office Communication**
    Write communication about return to office policy: [describe policy]. Address concerns and set clear expectations.

50. **Workplace Incident Report**
    Document a workplace incident: [describe]. Include facts, witnesses, immediate actions, and follow-up needed.
