# Presentation Creation Prompts

## Pitch Decks

1. **Startup Pitch Deck Outline**
   Create a 10-slide pitch deck outline for [company/product]. Include problem, solution, market size, business model, traction, team, and ask. Follow the standard VC format.

2. **Sales Pitch Deck Structure**
   Structure a sales pitch deck for [product/service] targeting [customer type]. Lead with their pain points, show solution, proof points, and clear next steps.

3. **Partnership Pitch Deck**
   Create a pitch deck for proposing a partnership with [company]. Cover mutual benefits, synergies, proposed structure, and what we're asking for.

4. **Investor Update Deck**
   Structure a monthly/quarterly investor update deck. Include key metrics, progress highlights, challenges, cash position, and asks for help.

5. **Internal Pitch Deck**
   Create a deck to pitch [initiative/idea] to internal stakeholders. Cover the problem, proposed solution, resources needed, expected outcomes, and risks.

## Business Presentations

6. **Quarterly Business Review Deck**
   Structure a QBR presentation for [client/internal]. Include performance vs goals, wins, learnings, and forward-looking strategy.

7. **Department Update Presentation**
   Create a department update presentation for [all-hands/leadership meeting]. Cover accomplishments, current focus, team updates, and upcoming priorities.

8. **Strategic Planning Presentation**
   Structure a strategic planning presentation for [planning period]. Include current state assessment, vision, goals, initiatives, and implementation roadmap.

9. **Budget Presentation**
   Create a budget presentation for [department/project]. Cover proposed allocations, justifications, expected outcomes, and comparison to prior periods.

10. **Year-End Review Presentation**
    Structure a year-end review presentation for [team/department]. Cover achievements, metrics, learnings, recognition, and preview of next year.

## Educational & Training Presentations

11. **Training Workshop Slides**
    Create a slide structure for a training workshop on [topic]. Include learning objectives, content sections, activities, and summary/next steps.

12. **Onboarding Presentation**
    Structure an onboarding presentation for new [employees/team members/customers]. Cover essential information in an engaging, memorable way.

13. **Best Practices Presentation**
    Create a presentation on [topic] best practices for [audience]. Include clear recommendations, examples, and practical takeaways.

14. **Process Training Deck**
    Structure a presentation teaching [process/system] to [audience]. Include step-by-step instructions, screenshots/visuals needed, and common mistakes to avoid.

15. **Lunch & Learn Presentation**
    Create a casual but informative lunch & learn presentation on [topic]. Make it engaging, include interactive elements, and respect time limits.

## Data & Analysis Presentations

16. **Data Presentation Structure**
    Structure a presentation of [data/analysis findings] for [audience]. Lead with insights, support with data, make visualizations clear, and end with implications.

17. **Research Findings Presentation**
    Create a presentation for research findings on [topic]. Cover methodology, key findings, implications, limitations, and recommended next steps.

18. **A/B Test Results Presentation**
    Structure a presentation on A/B test results for [test]. Cover hypothesis, setup, results, statistical confidence, and recommendations.

19. **Market Analysis Presentation**
    Create a market analysis presentation on [market/opportunity]. Include sizing, trends, competitive landscape, and strategic implications.

20. **Performance Dashboard Presentation**
    Structure a presentation walking through [dashboard/metrics]. Explain what to look at, how to interpret, trends to note, and actions to take.

## Project Presentations

21. **Project Kickoff Presentation**
    Create a project kickoff presentation for [project]. Cover objectives, scope, timeline, team roles, communication plan, and immediate next steps.

22. **Project Update Presentation**
    Structure a project status presentation for [stakeholder audience]. Include progress, accomplishments, risks/issues, and decisions needed.

23. **Project Proposal Presentation**
    Create a presentation proposing [project/initiative]. Cover problem, proposed solution, approach, timeline, resources, and expected outcomes.

24. **Project Retrospective Presentation**
    Structure a retrospective presentation for [completed project]. Cover what went well, what could improve, key learnings, and recommendations.

25. **Go-Live Readiness Presentation**
    Create a go-live readiness presentation for [project/launch]. Cover checklist status, remaining items, risk assessment, and recommendation to proceed.

## Persuasive Presentations

26. **Change Management Presentation**
    Structure a presentation introducing [change] to [affected stakeholders]. Address the why, what, how, and concerns proactively.

27. **Problem-Solution Presentation**
    Create a presentation following problem-solution structure for [topic]. Make the problem vivid, solution clear, and call to action specific.

28. **Recommendation Presentation**
    Structure a presentation recommending [action/decision]. Lead with recommendation, support with evidence, address objections, and request specific approval.

29. **Buy-In Presentation**
    Create a presentation to get buy-in for [initiative] from [stakeholders]. Understand their priorities and frame accordingly.

30. **Skeptic-Friendly Presentation**
    Structure a presentation for an audience skeptical about [topic]. Acknowledge concerns, provide compelling evidence, and invite dialogue.

## Slide Content

31. **Title Slide Copy**
    Write compelling title slide content for a presentation about [topic]. Include subtitle that communicates the key message.

32. **Opening Slide Hook**
    Create an opening slide that hooks the audience for a presentation about [topic]. Use a statistic, question, story, or provocative statement.

33. **Agenda Slide Content**
    Write an agenda slide for a [duration] presentation covering [topics]. Make it useful for audience orientation, not just a bulleted list.

34. **Key Message Slides**
    For each of these points, write a slide title and 3-4 bullets: [list main points]. Titles should be full sentences stating the takeaway.

35. **Data Slide Headlines**
    Write headlines for slides presenting this data: [paste data]. Headlines should state the insight, not just describe the chart.

36. **Transition Slides**
    Write transition slide content connecting [Section A] to [Section B]. Summarize where we've been and preview where we're going.

37. **Call-to-Action Slide**
    Create a closing slide with clear call-to-action for [presentation topic]. Make the ask specific and the next step obvious.

38. **Q&A Slide Alternatives**
    Suggest 3 alternatives to a basic "Q&A" slide for ending a presentation. Make them more engaging and memorable.

39. **Appendix Organization**
    Organize these supporting materials into an appendix: [list materials]. Create section headers and logical flow.

40. **Speaker Notes**
    Write speaker notes for this slide: [paste slide content]. Include what to say, timing, and any verbal bridges to next slide.

## Presentation Enhancement

41. **Slide Deck Review**
    Review this presentation and suggest improvements: [paste or describe deck]. Focus on flow, clarity, visual suggestions, and message effectiveness.

42. **Simplifying Complex Slides**
    Simplify this complex slide: [paste slide content]. Reduce cognitive load while preserving the key message.

43. **Adding Storytelling**
    Suggest how to add narrative/storytelling to this presentation: [describe current deck]. Identify opportunity for story arc.

44. **Presentation Timing**
    This presentation has [X] slides for [Y] minutes. Suggest timing per section and identify what to cut or condense if needed.

45. **Audience Adaptation**
    Adapt this presentation from [original audience] to [new audience]: [describe presentation]. Adjust framing, detail level, and emphasis.

## Preparation & Delivery

46. **Presentation Preparation Checklist**
    Create a preparation checklist for presenting [presentation type] to [audience]. Cover content, logistics, and mindset preparation.

47. **Opening Script**
    Write an opening script for my presentation on [topic] to [audience]. Hook attention, establish credibility, and preview content.

48. **Closing Script**
    Write a closing script that reinforces key messages and ends strong. Include call-to-action and memorable final statement.

49. **Anticipated Questions**
    Generate 10 likely questions from [audience] about this presentation: [describe content]. Include challenging questions and draft concise answers.

50. **Presentation Self-Assessment**
    Create a self-assessment rubric for evaluating my presentation on: structure, content, visuals, delivery, and audience engagement.
